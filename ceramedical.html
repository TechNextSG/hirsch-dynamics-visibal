Manufacturing Capabilities

What Ceramedical Delivers

Pushing the boundaries of ceramic micro-manufacturing for medical, dental, and industrial applications worldwide.

CIM — Ceramic Injection Molding

Complex geometries in zirconia and alumina. Net-shape components with ±0.3µm tolerances and excellent surface finish.

MIM — Metal Injection Molding

High-density metal micro-components from stainless steel, titanium, and specialty alloys. Tight dimensional control for critical assemblies.

Advanced Material Portfolio

Zirconia (3Y-TZP), alumina, PEEK, titanium, and stainless steel — matched to the biocompatibility and mechanical requirements of each application.

ISO 13485 Cleanroom Facility

Production in a certified cleanroom environment meeting the strictest requirements for medical device manufacturing and contamination control.

µm-Range Tolerances

Dimensional accuracy at the micrometer level — essential for implant components, surgical instruments, and precision dental fittings.

Full Regulatory Compliance

CE MDR 2017/745 and ISO 13485 certified. Every batch is traceable, documented, and audit-ready for global distribution.

Technical Specifications

Precision at Every Level

  • Processes: CIM (Ceramic Injection Molding), MIM (Metal Injection Molding), post-sintering finishing
  • Materials: Zirconia (3Y-TZP), alumina (Al₂O₃), PEEK, 316L stainless steel, titanium alloys
  • Tolerance: ±0.3µm achievable on critical dimensions — consistent across production runs
  • Facility: ISO Class 7 cleanroom; full environmental monitoring and batch documentation

  • Volume: Prototyping through high-volume series production; scalable tooling investment
Manufacturing Process

From Powder to
Certified Component

Five tightly controlled stages — each with full documentation and traceability for ISO 13485 and FDA compliance.

1
Feedstock
ZrO₂ / Al₂O₃

Ceramic powder prepared and compounded with binder system

2
Micro Injection
1200 bar · 215°C

High-pressure injection into precision-machined steel tooling

3
Debinding
Thermal / Catalytic · 400°C

Binder removed thermally or catalytically under controlled atmosphere

4
Sintering
1450°C

Final densification to >99% theoretical density in high-temp furnace

5
QC · CMM
100% Inspection · ±0.3µm

Every component measured by CMM with traceable batch reports
